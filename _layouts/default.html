Research

My research focus is on computationally feasible learning-based methods that learn from a limited number of (possibly noisy) samples. I am interested in investigating the limits of such methods and their approximations from a theoretical and practical standpoint.

Currently, I am researching on learning-based methods for autonomous power management of energy-constrained devices such as energy-harvesting wireless sensor nodes. Consquently, I am involved in reinforcement learning and its relation to neural network function approximation, off-policy learning and distributed learning.

During my lectureship in SEC, I kickstarted a research initiative into FPGAs and HDL programming, the first of its kind in Nepal. I established Xilinx Research Laboratory (XRLab) in collaboration with Deepesh Man Shakya of Xilinx Inc., Ireland. I also coordinated introductory FPGA and HDL training sessions for electronics and computer engineering undergraduates with Sujit Chhetri, Sandesh Ghimire, Bikash Poudel and Prasanna Kansakar.



Publications

My most recent publications are focused on applying reinforcement learning (RL) for autonomous power management of energy-harvesting wireless sensor nodes. My research was supported by the Japanese Government MEXT Scholarship from 2015-2018 and by JSPS DC1 Research Fellowship since 2018.
